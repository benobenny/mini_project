Transport in the City

Overview of London's Transport Network

London boasts one of the world's most comprehensive and efficient public transport systems, making it easy to navigate the city. Whether you're traveling by the iconic red buses, hopping on the Underground, or cycling through scenic streets, London offers something for everyone.

Types of Transport

The Tube (London Underground)

The London Underground has 11 lines, covering the entire city. Key stations include King's Cross, Paddington, and Waterloo. Trains run from around 5:30 AM to midnight, with some lines offering 24-hour service on weekends.

Buses

London's iconic red buses are a great way to explore the city. With over 700 routes, they offer a scenic and affordable travel option. Night buses are also available for late-night travelers.

Trains

National Rail services connect Greater London and beyond. Major stations include London Bridge, Victoria, and Liverpool Street. These are ideal for day trips to nearby destinations.

Taxis and Ride-Sharing

Black cabs and ride-sharing apps like Uber and Bolt provide convenient door-to-door service. Black cabs can be hailed on the street or booked at taxi ranks.

Cycling

Boris Bikes (Santander Cycles) are available for hire throughout the city. London also has dedicated cycle lanes for a safer riding experience.

Walking

Many of London's top attractions are within walking distance of each other, making it a pedestrian-friendly city. Explore areas like Covent Garden and Soho on foot.

Key Information for Travelers

Use an Oyster Card or contactless payment for the cheapest fares.

Travel off-peak (after 9:30 AM) to save money and avoid crowds.

Download the TfL Go app for real-time updates and journey planning.

You cannot pay for bus journeys in London with cash

Instead, you can pay for your bus journey in London with a Visitor Oyster card. Simply touch your card on the reader beside the driver as you get on the bus. The fee is always the same, £1.50 per journey

If you travel only on buses, a Visitor Oyster card will limit your daily spend at £4.50 per day. If you also travel on the tube on the same day, the price limit will be higher (depending on zones and time of day - see more detailed information here)

You can also pay for your bus journey with a Day Travelcard. If you have a Travelcard, you can just show it to the driver as you get on the bus. Buses are not zone-specific so you can travel in any area of London with your Travelcard; it will not cost you any extra

There are hundreds of bus routes in London. Most bus stops display a full route map of every bus that stops there

You can only catch a bus from designated bus stops. Only the numbered buses listed on the bus stop will stop there

There are downloadable bus maps grouped by area on the Transport for London website, which also links them with nearby tube stations

You can use Transport for London’s journey planner to find your way around by bus - just select ‘bus only’ as the mode of transport

Avoid traveling during rush hours (7:30-9:30 AM and 5:00-7:00 PM) to enjoy a smoother journey.
